### Lesson Description

Lesson 04 introduces students to the complete water cycle—how water continuously moves around Earth. The lesson breaks down this complex natural process into four digestible stages: evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection. Each stage is explained with real-world observations students can witness in their daily lives, building vocabulary and understanding of Earth's essential water systems.

The lesson follows the established structure of lessons 01, 02, and 03, maintaining consistency in pedagogy, formatting, visual design, and educational scaffolding.

## Educational Objectives

### Learning Targets

Students will be able to:

1. **Explain evaporation** – Describe what evaporation is and identify when it happens in nature.
2. **Describe condensation** – Explain how water vapor turns into liquid water droplets and forms clouds.
3. **Identify precipitation** – Name forms of precipitation (rain, snow, sleet, hail) and explain why precipitation is important.
4. **Trace the water cycle** – Follow the path of water through all stages of the complete cycle.

### Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Evaporation** | When water turns from a liquid into a gas (vapor) and rises into the air. |
| **Condensation** | When water vapor cools and turns back into liquid water droplets. |
| **Precipitation** | Water that falls from clouds to Earth (rain, snow, sleet, or hail). |
| **Collection** | When water gathers in oceans, lakes, rivers, and the ground. |

## Lesson Structure

### Section Breakdown

The lesson contains **5 sections** following the established pattern:

#### **Section 1: What is the water cycle?** (Overview)
- **Focus:** Introduction to the water cycle concept
- **Key Idea:** Water is always moving in a never-ending cycle
- **Quick Facts:** Covers billions of years of cycling, movement between sky/land/oceans, sun's role
- **Look Closely:** Observation prompts about puddles, clouds, and water movement
- **Callout:** "The water cycle never stops—it's always happening, even on cloudy days."
- **Prompt A:** Describe where a water drop travels (2–3 sentences)

#### **Section 2: Evaporation: water rises into the air** (Stage 1)
- **Focus:** How the sun heats water and creates water vapor
- **Key Idea:** Sun → heat → water becomes invisible gas that rises
- **Quick Facts:** Sun provides heat energy, liquid becomes vapor, vapor is invisible
- **Look Closely:** Feel sun warmth, observe puddles disappearing, see clothes drying
- **Prompt B:** Explain evaporation and identify two places where it occurs (fill both lines)

#### **Section 3: Condensation: water vapor forms clouds** (Stage 2)
- **Focus:** How cooling water vapor becomes clouds
- **Key Idea:** Rising vapor cools → becomes liquid droplets → forms clouds
- **Quick Facts:** Clouds made of billions of droplets, vapor turns to liquid when cool, opposite of evaporation
- **Look Closely:** Examine clouds, observe visible breath in cold, identify fog
- **Prompt C:** Explain cloud formation process (3–4 sentences)

#### **Section 4: Precipitation: water falls to Earth** (Stage 3)
- **Focus:** How water returns to Earth in different forms
- **Key Idea:** Droplets combine, get heavy, fall as different types of precipitation depending on temperature
- **Quick Facts:** Describes rain, snow, sleet, and hail
- **Think Like a Scientist:** Why does it fall? What determines type? Where does it land?
- **Prompt D:** Compare rain and snow, explain why different types occur in different seasons (one paragraph)

#### **Section 5: Collection & why the cycle matters** (Stage 4 + Importance)
- **Focus:** Where water collects and why the cycle matters
- **Key Idea:** Water collects and eventually evaporates again—continuous cycle forever
- **Collection Locations:** Oceans (97% of water), lakes/rivers, groundwater
- **Why It Matters:** Provides fresh water, distributes heat, shapes weather/climate
- **Prompt E (Summary):** Describe complete cycle using all 4 vocabulary words (5–6 sentences)
- **Prompt F (Application):** Track one water molecule through all 5 stages (bullet format)
