Plants & Animals

This page is designed like a premium educational website — but when you print it, it becomes a clean worksheet. Read each section, study the images, then answer in the writing boxes (type on screen or write on paper).

Section 1

What makes something a living thing?

Living things have needs and can do certain jobs to stay alive. Many living things grow, use energy, respond to changes, and make more of their kind.

Quick Facts

  • Plants make their own food using sunlight.
  • Animals must eat plants or other animals.
  • All living things need water and energy.

Look Closely

  • Find a leaf, stem, and flower (or bud).
  • What clues show the plant is alive?
  • Where might it get water?
Prompt A — Living or Not? 2–3 sentences
✔ Key idea: Living things grow, use energy, respond to changes, and reproduce (plants also make food from sunlight).
Section 2

What do plants and animals need?

Plants and animals both need water and energy. Animals also need food. Plants need sunlight to make food. Many animals need air to breathe.

Website on screen, worksheet on paper: these writing boxes work both ways.

Plants Need

  • Sunlight
  • Water
  • Air (carbon dioxide)
  • Space to grow

Animals Need

  • Food
  • Water
  • Air (oxygen)
  • Shelter
Prompt B — Compare Fill both lines
✔ Key idea: Shared needs include water/energy; difference: plants need sunlight to make food, animals must eat.
Section 3

Habitats: a living thing’s home

A habitat is the place where a plant or animal lives. A good habitat provides food, water, shelter, and space.

Vocabulary

  • Habitat: where it lives
  • Shelter: safe place to hide/rest
  • Resource: something it needs

Image Mission

  • Name 2 things you see.
  • On paper: circle where shelter might be.
  • What food could exist here?
Prompt C — Habitat Report 3–4 sentences
✔ Key idea: Habitat provides resources: food, water, shelter, and space (use visible evidence).
Section 4

Adaptations: helpful body parts or behaviors

An adaptation is something that helps a living thing survive. It can be a body part (like sharp claws) or a behavior (like migrating). Adaptations match the habitat.

Examples

  • Thick fur keeps animals warm.
  • Camouflage helps hide from predators.
  • Waxy leaves help plants save water.

Think Like a Scientist

  • What problem does the habitat create?
  • What feature solves that problem?
  • How does it help survival?
Prompt D — Match & Explain One paragraph
✔ Key idea: Adaptation = trait/behavior that improves survival (tie it to habitat problem + solution).
Section 5

Quick Check + Create

Finish strong. Use the words needs, habitat, and adaptation.

Mini Quiz

  • Define habitat in your own words.
  • Name 2 needs all living things share.
  • What is an adaptation?

Challenge

  • Invent a new animal.
  • Choose its habitat.
  • Give it 2 adaptations.
Prompt E — Summary 5–6 sentences
✔ Key idea: Summary includes shared needs (water/energy), habitat resources, and adaptations that match habitat.
Prompt F — Create an Animal Bullets OK
✔ Key idea: Adaptations must connect to habitat challenges (cold, heat, predators, low water, etc.).
