Ecosystems & Interdependence

Ecosystems & Interdependence • Printable Web Lesson

On screen this looks like a modern lesson page — but when you print it, it becomes a worksheet. Read, observe, then write your explanations using evidence from the images and quick facts.

What Students Will Learn

Students learn what an ecosystem is, how living and nonliving parts work together, and why living things depend on each other. They explore interdependence (like pollination), what happens when ecosystems change, and how to design their own ecosystem using resources, relationships, and survival needs.

Learning Targets

  • I can explain what an ecosystem is (living and nonliving parts together).
  • I can identify biotic (living) and abiotic (nonliving) factors.
  • I can describe how living things depend on each other for survival.
  • I can explain how changes can affect an ecosystem.

Student Directions

  • Read each section and study the picture.
  • Use evidence from the quick facts to answer.
  • Write complete sentences when asked.
  • On paper: underline key vocabulary words.

Key Vocabulary

Ecosystem
a community of living things and the nonliving parts of their environment working together.
Biotic
living parts of an ecosystem (plants, animals, fungi, bacteria).
Abiotic
nonliving parts of an ecosystem (sunlight, water, air, soil, rocks, temperature).
Interdependence
when living things depend on each other to survive.

Section 1: What is an ecosystem?

An ecosystem includes all the living things in an area and all the nonliving parts they interact with—like water, air, sunlight, and soil.

Quick Facts

  • Ecosystems can be large (rainforests) or small (a pond).
  • Living things need nonliving resources to survive.
  • Ecosystems include plants, animals, and many other organisms.

Look Closely

  • Name 3 living things that could live here.
  • Name 3 nonliving things you notice or can infer.
  • What makes this place a good home?

Prompt A — Define + Evidence

2–3 sentences

Explain why this scene is an ecosystem. Name one living thing and one nonliving thing, and explain how they connect.

Section 2: Biotic vs. Abiotic factors

Biotic factors are living things. Abiotic factors are nonliving parts like soil, water, sunlight, and temperature. Both affect survival.

Biotic (Living)

  • Plants
  • Animals
  • Fungi
  • Bacteria (tiny living things)

Abiotic (Nonliving)

  • Soil and rocks
  • Water
  • Air
  • Sunlight and temperature

Use the picture to support your choices—don’t guess without evidence.

Prompt B — Sort & Explain

Fill both lines

One BIOTIC factor in this picture is: ____________ One ABIOTIC factor in this picture is: ____________ Explain how the abiotic factor helps the biotic factor survive: ____________________________

Section 3: Interdependence: living things need each other

Many living things depend on each other for food, shelter, protection, or reproduction. Pollination is one example.

Quick Facts

  • Bees collect nectar for food.
  • As bees move, they transfer pollen between flowers.
  • Pollination helps plants make seeds and fruit.

Look Closely

  • What is the bee getting from the flower?
  • How is the flower helped by the bee?
  • Why is this relationship important?

Prompt C —Interdependence

3–4 sentences

Describe how the bee and the flower depend on each other. What does each one get from this relationship?

Section 4: What happens when ecosystems change?

When an abiotic factor changes—like water, temperature, or storms—living things may struggle to survive. Some move, adapt, or die.

Examples

  • Less rain → fewer plants grow
  • Fewer plants → fewer animals can find food
  • Extreme heat or cold → some animals migrate

Think Like a Scientist

  • What nonliving factor might have changed here?
  • Which living things would be affected first?
  • What could happen next in the ecosystem?

Prompt D — Cause & Effect

One paragraph

Imagine this area gets much less water for a long time. Explain what could happen to plants first, then animals. Use step-by-step reasoning.

Section 5: Quick Check + Create

Use the words ecosystem, biotic, abiotic, and interdependence.

Mini Quiz

  • What is an ecosystem?
  • Name 2 abiotic factors.
  • What does interdependence mean?

Challenge

  • Choose an ecosystem type (reef, desert, forest, pond).
  • List 2 biotic parts and 2 abiotic parts.
  • Describe one way living things depend on each other.

Prompt E — Summary

5–6 sentences

Write a summary explaining what an ecosystem is, how biotic and abiotic factors work together, and why interdependence matters.

Prompt F — Build Your Own Ecosystem

Bullets OK
