Science Lessons

Life science, earth science, and printable observation lessons.

  • Plants & Animals

    In this lesson, students explore what makes something a living thing, what plants and animals need to survive, how habitats provide resources, and how adaptations help living things solve problems in their environment. Each section includes quick facts, a “look closely” mission, and a writing prompt that builds science vocabulary and complete-sentence explanations.

  • Food Chains

    Students learn how energy moves through a food chain: producers make food, consumers eat plants or animals, and decomposers break down dead material. Each section builds vocabulary and explanation skills.

  • Ecosystems & Interdependence

    Students learn what an ecosystem is, how living and nonliving parts work together, and why living things depend on each other. They explore interdependence (like pollination), what happens when ecosystems change, and how to design their own ecosystem using resources, relationships, and survival needs.

  • The Water Cycle

    In this lesson, students explore how water moves around Earth in a continuous cycle. They learn about evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection—the four main steps that keep water moving through our environment. Each section includes quick facts, observation missions, and writing prompts that build science vocabulary and explain how Earth's water stays in motion.

  • Trees

    In this lesson, students explore what makes a tree a tree, learn the names and jobs of each tree part, discover how trees use sunlight, water, and air to grow, and find out how trees help animals and people every day. Each section includes quick facts, observation missions, and writing prompts that build science vocabulary and complete-sentence explanations.

  • Habitats

    In this lesson, students explore what a habitat is and why habitats matter. They discover that every living thing needs food, water, shelter, and space to survive. Students will explore three very different habitats — forests, oceans, and deserts — and learn how animals and plants are adapted to each one. The lesson ends with why protecting habitats is important for all living things, including us.
