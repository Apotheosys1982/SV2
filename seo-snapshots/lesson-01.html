Plants & Animals

Plants + Animals • Printable Web Lesson

This page is designed like a premium educational website — but when you print it, it becomes a clean worksheet. Read each section, study the images, then answer in the writing boxes (type on screen or write on paper).

What Students Will Learn

In this lesson, students explore what makes something a living thing, what plants and animals need to survive, how habitats provide resources, and how adaptations help living things solve problems in their environment. Each section includes quick facts, a “look closely” mission, and a writing prompt that builds science vocabulary and complete-sentence explanations.

Learning Targets

  • I can describe clues that show something is living.
  • I can compare what plants need and what animals need.
  • I can explain what a habitat provides (food, water, shelter, space).
  • I can connect an adaptation to a habitat challenge.

Student Directions

  • Read the section and study the image.
  • Use the “Quick Facts” to support your answers.
  • Write in complete sentences when asked.
  • On paper: you may circle, label, or underline key details.

Key Vocabulary

Living thing
something that grows, uses energy, and needs resources.
Needs
resources required to survive (water, energy, food, air, space).
Habitat
the place a plant or animal lives.
Adaptation
a body part or behavior that helps survival.

Section 1: What makes something a living thing?

Living things have needs and can do certain jobs to stay alive. Many living things grow, use energy, respond to changes, and make more of their kind.

Quick Facts

  • Plants make their own food using sunlight.
  • Animals must eat plants or other animals.
  • All living things need water and energy.

Look Closely

  • Find a leaf, stem, and flower (or bud).
  • What clues show the plant is alive?
  • Where might it get water?

Prompt A — Living or Not?

2–3 sentences

Explain how you know the thing in the picture is alive. Use at least two clues.

Section 2: What do plants and animals need?

Plants and animals both need water and energy. Animals also need food. Plants need sunlight to make food. Many animals need air to breathe.

Plants Need

  • Sunlight
  • Water
  • Air (carbon dioxide)
  • Space to grow

Animals Need

  • Food
  • Water
  • Air (oxygen)
  • Shelter

Website on screen, worksheet on paper: these writing boxes work both ways.

Prompt B — Compare

Fill both lines

1) One need plants and animals share is: ______________________ 2) One need that is different is: _____________________________

Section 3: Habitats: a living thing’s home

A habitat is the place where a plant or animal lives. A good habitat provides food, water, shelter, and space.

Vocabulary

  • Habitat: where it lives
  • Shelter: safe place to hide/rest
  • Resource: something it needs

Image Mission

  • Name 2 things you see.
  • On paper: circle where shelter might be.
  • What food could exist here?

Prompt C — Habitat Report

3–4 sentences

Describe this habitat. What resources could help plants and animals survive here?

Section 4: Adaptations: helpful body parts or behaviors

An adaptation is something that helps a living thing survive. It can be a body part (like sharp claws) or a behavior (like migrating). Adaptations match the habitat.

Examples

  • Thick fur keeps animals warm.
  • Camouflage helps hide from predators.
  • Waxy leaves help plants save water.

Think Like a Scientist

  • What problem does the habitat create?
  • What feature solves that problem?
  • How does it help survival?

Prompt D — Match & Explain

One paragraph

Choose one plant OR one animal. Name one adaptation and explain how it helps in its habitat.

Section 5: Quick Check + Create

Finish strong. Use the words needs, habitat, and adaptation.

Mini Quiz

  • Define habitat in your own words.
  • Name 2 needs all living things share.
  • What is an adaptation?

Challenge

  • Invent a new animal.
  • Choose its habitat.
  • Give it 2 adaptations.

Prompt E — Summary

5–6 sentences

Write a short summary: needs, habitats, and adaptations. Use all three words.

Prompt F — Create an Animal

Bullets OK

Name: ____________ Habitat: ____________ Adaptation #1: ____________ (helps by ________) Adaptation #2: ____________ (helps by ________)
