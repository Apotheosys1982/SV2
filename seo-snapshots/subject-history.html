History Lessons

World history and American history lessons built around evidence, timelines, communities, and civic ideas.

  • History Detectives

    Students learn how historians use timelines, artifacts, photographs, maps, letters, and other primary sources to ask questions and build explanations about the past.

  • Rivers, Cities & Early America

    Students explore why rivers helped communities grow, how ancient Egypt and early cities used land and writing, and how Indigenous communities in North America used deep knowledge of local places.

  • American Independence & World Ideas

    Students learn about colonies, the American Revolution, American symbols, and how ideas about fairness, rights, responsibility, and voice connect to people in many places.
