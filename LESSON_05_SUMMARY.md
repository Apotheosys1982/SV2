### Lesson Description

Lesson 05 introduces students to trees — what they are, their parts, how they grow, and why they matter to animals and people. The lesson covers roots, trunk, branches, leaves, photosynthesis (in age-appropriate terms), habitats, and the many ways trees support life on Earth. Each section includes quick facts, observation missions, and scaffolded writing prompts.

---

## Educational Objectives

### Learning Targets

Students will be able to:

1. **Describe what a tree is** and name its main parts.
2. **Explain the jobs** of roots, trunk, branches, and leaves.
3. **Describe how trees use** sunlight, water, air, and soil to grow.
4. **Explain at least three ways** trees help animals and people.

### Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Roots** | The part of the tree underground that soaks up water and holds the tree in place. |
| **Trunk** | The thick stem of the tree that holds it up and carries water from the roots to the leaves. |
| **Leaves** | The flat green parts of a tree that catch sunlight and make food for the tree. |
| **Photosynthesis** | The way a tree uses sunlight, water, and air to make its own food. |
| **Habitat** | A home in nature where an animal finds food, water, and shelter. |

## Lesson Structure

### Section 1: What Is a Tree?
- **Focus:** Introduction to trees as tall, long-living plants
- **Key Idea:** Trees are big plants with four main parts
- **Quick Facts:** 60,000+ tree species, four main parts, alive and growing
- **Prompt A:** Observe and describe what makes a tree different from a small plant

### Section 2: Parts of a Tree
- **Focus:** Roots, trunk, branches, and leaves — each with a job
- **Key Idea:** Every part of a tree has an important function
- **Quick Facts:** Roots drink, trunk carries, branches hold, leaves make food
- **Prompt B:** Name the parts and their jobs (fill-in-the-blank)

### Section 3: How Trees Grow
- **Focus:** Sunlight, water, air, soil, and photosynthesis
- **Key Idea:** Trees make their own food using photosynthesis
- **Quick Facts:** Photosynthesis needs sunlight + water + air; trees grow faster in spring/summer
- **Prompt C:** Explain photosynthesis in 3–4 sentences

### Section 4: How Trees Help Animals
- **Focus:** Trees as habitats — shelter, food, nesting
- **Key Idea:** A tree is a whole neighborhood for animals
- **Quick Facts:** Birds nest, squirrels hide food, insects shelter in bark
- **Prompt D:** Describe a tree as a habitat (one paragraph)

### Section 5: How Trees Help People
- **Focus:** Oxygen, shade, wood products, beauty, clean air
- **Key Idea:** Trees are essential for human life and communities
- **Quick Facts:** Oxygen production, shade, wood for building, air cleaning
- **Prompt E:** Explain three ways trees help people (5–6 sentences)
- **Prompt F:** Personal connection — describe a real tree the student knows
