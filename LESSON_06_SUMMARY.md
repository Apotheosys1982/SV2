## Lesson Overview

**Title:** Habitats
**Subtitle:** Where Living Things Find Food, Water, and Shelter
**ID:** lesson-06
**Target Audience:** Grades 2–4 (ages 7–10)
**Duration:** 20–30 min
**Format:** Printable web lesson (website on screen, worksheet on paper)

This lesson extends the existing SV2 science cluster (Plants & Animals, Food Chains, Ecosystems, Water Cycle, Trees) by focusing on habitats — the natural homes where living things find everything they need to survive.

---

## Educational Objectives

Students will learn:

1. **What a habitat is** — a natural home that provides food, water, shelter, and space
2. **Forest habitats** — how trees, birds, squirrels, and insects depend on the forest
3. **Ocean habitats** — how sea turtles, fish, and coral reefs form underwater ecosystems
4. **Desert habitats** — how living things adapt to extreme heat and water scarcity
5. **Why habitats matter** — conservation, pollution, and human responsibility

---

## Lesson Structure

### Section 1 — What Is a Habitat?
- Defines habitat and the four survival needs (food, water, shelter, space)
- Image: Zebras at a waterhole (savanna habitat)
- Prompt A: Observe a habitat (3–4 sentences)

### Section 2 — Forest Habitats
- Explores forest ecosystems: canopy, forest floor, food sources
- Image: Squirrel in a sunlit forest
- Prompt B: Forest report (3–4 sentences)

### Section 3 — Ocean Habitats
- Covers coral reefs, sea turtles, ocean food chains
- Image: Sea turtle swimming through coral reef
- Prompt C: Ocean food chain (4–5 sentences, uses producer/consumer)

### Section 4 — Desert Habitats
- Examines desert adaptations: cacti, reptiles, nocturnal behavior
- Image: Desert landscape with tall cacti
- Prompt D: Desert adaptations (one paragraph, 4–5 sentences)

### Section 5 — Why Habitats Matter
- Conservation, pollution, human impact, and solutions
- Image: Elephants in the South African savanna
- Prompt E: Habitat hero (5–6 sentences, uses conservation)

---

## Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Habitat | The natural home of a plant or animal, where it finds food, water, shelter, and space. |
| Shelter | A safe place where an animal can hide, rest, and stay protected from weather and predators. |
| Adaptation | A body part or behavior that helps a living thing survive in its habitat. |
| Ecosystem | A community of living things and the nonliving parts of their environment working together. |
| Conservation | Protecting nature and keeping habitats safe for the plants and animals that live there. |

## Prompt Progression

The prompts follow a scaffolded progression:

1. **Observe** (Section 1) — Describe what you see in a habitat
2. **Describe** (Section 2) — Report on a specific habitat
3. **Explain** (Section 3) — Trace energy through a food chain using scientific terms
4. **Apply** (Section 4) — Choose an organism and explain its adaptations
5. **Synthesize** (Section 5) — Write about why habitats matter and how people can help

All prompts are solvable by students in Grades 2–4 and align with the learning targets.
